What is gōster?

gōster is a real-time clinical writing assistant designed for radiologists. It works like a medical ghostwriter: you dictate findings with short phrases (triggers) and gōster generates the complete report, structured and with professional radiological terminology.

The name comes from ghostwriter, with the O as a visual mark representing the absent H. gōster works silently so that the report speaks for you.

Think of gōster as your intelligent transcriptionist You do not need to dictate in a structured way or follow a specific order. You can speak naturally — as if you were discussing the case with a colleague or thinking the findings aloud. gōster listens, analyzes your voice in real time, and converts that natural conversation into a structured, complete radiological report with professional terminology. Forget the tedium of drafting: spend your time on what truly matters — analyzing your studies in depth. gōster handles the rest.

Sign up and log in

gōster uses a user account system. Each user has their own workspace with independent dictionaries, templates, and history.

Sign up

  • Email and password — Enter your name, email, and a password (minimum 6 characters).
  • Google — Click "Sign in with Google" to register automatically.

Log in

  • Email and password — Enter the credentials you used to sign up.
  • Google — A single click if you already have a linked account.
  • Smooth transition — When you log in, the screen fades softly and a chord plays confirming access.

Reset password

Click "Forgot your password?" on the login screen. Enter your email and you will receive a recovery link.

Initial setup

Before generating your first report, complete these two steps.

Step 1 — Upload your study templates

Go to the Templates section and upload one template for each type of study.

  • Accepted formats: DOCX, DOC, or PDF.
  • You can also create templates manually with pasted text.
  • gōster automatically detects the type of study.
  • You can edit the study name and content from its card.
Tip Upload a fully normal report for each modality. The more complete the template, the better the results.

Step 2 — Build your clinical dictionary

Go to the Dictionary section and upload your previous reports of pathological studies.

  • Conclusions — each finding becomes a trigger phrase.
  • Descriptive section — the detailed clinical description is associated with its trigger.

If you already have a dictionary for the same type of study, gōster merges new entries without duplicating.

Note — Privacy gōster automatically detects and anonymizes patient data.

Dictionary management

Book icon and quick activation

Each card shows a closed-book icon on the left. Click to activate/deactivate.

Note When you activate a Template, gōster automatically links the dictionary that best matches.

View and edit entries

Click "X entries" to expand. Each entry shows:

  • Trigger — the short phrase you dictate.
  • Description(s) — the full clinical text that gōster will use.

Multiple description variants

The same trigger can have several descriptions. gōster chooses one at random, avoiding repetitive reports.

Add entries manually

At the end of the expanded list you will find the "+ Add entry" button.

Reorder entries and cards (drag and drop)

  • Entries: hold down the drag icon on the left.
  • Cards: hold down the drag icon in the upper-left corner.

Search dictionaries

When you have 2+ dictionaries, a search field appears. Type fragments of the name and gōster locates the card.

Automatic adaptation of measurements

When you upload a previous report, gōster extracts the triggers and descriptions without including the specific measurements from the original study. The descriptions are saved as generic, clean, reusable "recipes". You do not have to manipulate or edit anything.

When generating a new report, gōster takes the measurements you dictate and automatically inserts them into the corresponding description.

How it works
Original uploaded report "8 mm disc herniation with root compression at L4-L5"
What gōster saves Trigger: "disc herniation" — Description: "Disc herniation with root compression"
You dictate "12 mm disc herniation at L5-S1"
gōster generates "12 mm disc herniation with root compression at L5-S1"
You do not need to edit the descriptions. gōster takes care of adapting measurements, laterality, and levels automatically every time you generate a report. Just upload your reports and dictate.

Generate a report

Workflow

  1. Activate the template for the type of study. gōster will automatically link the dictionary.
  2. Go to the Report section and verify the context tags.
  3. Dictate or type your findings using your trigger phrases.
  4. Click "Generate report". The text appears in real time with a fluid progressive-writing animation. Use the speed control to adjust the streaming pace, and the pause button (next to the speed slider) to stop and resume generation when needed.
  5. When streaming finishes, an independent auditor (Agent 3) automatically validates the complete report. If it detects errors or inconsistencies, it corrects them transparently before showing you the final result.
  6. Review and confirm that the report is correct.
  7. Copy or download the final result.
Note When you log in, no dictionary or template is activated automatically.

What gōster does with the dictation

  • Flexible semantic interpretation — gōster prioritizes your clinical intent over exact textual matching. It recognizes language variations, dictation errors, incomplete phrases, synonyms, and changes in sentence order.
  • Intelligent dictionary matching — When there is a reasonable match between your dictation and a trigger, the dictionary description is automatically activated. It does not rely on exact matches: the matching is semantic.
  • It automatically adapts laterality, measurements, grades, percentages, and classifications from the dictation to the dictionary description.
  • If multiple descriptions exist for a trigger, it selects one variably while maintaining clinical coherence.
  • Sections not mentioned retain the normal text from the template.
  • If you say "rest normal", everything else is kept as in the template.
  • Real-time corrections — If you correct yourself ("oh no", "wait", "better change that", "remove that"), gōster immediately overwrites the previous version and maintains full coherence between description and conclusions.
  • Active learning — gōster consults your previous corrections and your report history as active memory, automatically applying the learned patterns.
  • gōster always generates the Diagnostic Impression, inferring conclusions from the body, dictionary, and history, ordered by clinical relevance.
  • Absolute fidelity — It does not change your words if there is no prior trigger or pattern justifying it.
  • Without an active dictionary, it uses its own clinical knowledge.
  • Global and special instructions are always applied.

Real-time corrections and intelligent trigger expansion

gōster understands that dictation is a dynamic process. If you correct yourself, change a measurement, or rephrase a finding during dictation, the system automatically discards the provisional values and keeps only your final decision.

Example: if you dictate "16-degree inclination... no wait, it's 19 degrees", gōster discards the 16 and uses 19 throughout the report (description, tables, and conclusions).

In addition, when you say a short trigger phrase, gōster automatically expands it to its full description. You do not need to dictate the long description: the system retrieves it from the dictionary or history.

Example: if you say "semimembranosus tendinosis" and your dictionary has that phrase associated with "increased thickness and signal intensity of the semimembranosus tendon", the report will use the expanded description, not the short phrase.

Dictate in short mode, gōster writes in correct mode. Use trigger phrases as shortcuts and correct yourself freely during dictation. gōster takes care of expanding, resolving, and producing a clean report with the final version of each finding.

Automatic semantic coherence

gōster internally validates each paragraph before displaying it to avoid contradictions. If the radiologist dictates a lesion finding, the model does not add automatic phrases of normality or preservation that contradict that finding.

Example of an error that gōster avoids:

  • "Anterior cruciate ligament thickened and hyperintense with diffuse partial tear... preserves the characteristic fascicular pattern."
  • Problem: a diffuse partial tear is not compatible with a preserved fascicular pattern. gōster detects this contradiction and automatically removes it.

Correct wording gōster produces:

  • "Anterior cruciate ligament thickened and hyperintense, with diffuse partial tear. Posterior cruciate ligament intact. Collateral ligaments without alterations."
Note gōster always prioritizes the dominant finding dictated by the radiologist. If it detects a conflict between lesion and integrity within the same structure, it removes the incompatible phrase and keeps the description faithful to the dictation.

Report editor, learning, and export

Persistent learning (Save changes)

gōster learns from your corrections. When you edit a generated report and click "Save changes", the following is recorded:

  • The original transcript you dictated.
  • The original report generated by the model.
  • The report corrected by you.
  • The active study type.

Each correction becomes permanent learning:

  • If you modify a description, gōster uses your version as the standard in future reports.
  • If you adjust a conclusion, it adopts that diagnostic form as preferred.
  • If you change terminology, it applies the new term going forward.
  • Transfer to similar cases: corrections are automatically applied to similar findings, even in different anatomical structures.

If you regenerate a report, all previous corrections are already reflected automatically, without you having to repeat them.

Tip Edit and save changes whenever you find something to improve. The more corrections you save, the faster gōster learns your style.

Voice correction assistant

After generating a report, a floating widget with the gōster ghost appears over the transcript panel. This assistant asks "Want to adjust anything?" and lets you correct the report without touching the keyboard:

  • Dictate correction: press the microphone button and dictate what you want to change (for example: "change the stenosis grade to moderate" or "add mild joint effusion"). gōster regenerates the report applying your instruction.
  • No, it's ready: closes the assistant if you do not need changes.

The widget can be dragged freely across the screen. It also has controls for:

  • Minimize: turns it into a compact square with the ghost. When minimized, it returns automatically to its original position. From there you can move it again or expand it with the + button.
  • Close: hides it completely.
Tip Use the correction assistant for quick adjustments without leaving the workflow. Dictate instructions in natural language as if speaking to a colleague: "remove the bursitis conclusion", "set the measurement to 3.2 cm", etc.

Fluid correction animation

When a correction is applied, whether from the voice assistant or the automatic auditor (Agent 3), the report does not change abruptly. Instead, gōster performs a smooth visual transition in three phases:

  1. Fade out: the current report gradually fades until invisible.
  2. Swap: while the content is hidden, it is replaced by the corrected version.
  3. Reappearance with pulse: the report reappears smoothly and the modified paragraphs are briefly highlighted with a pulse in the active accent color, so you can identify exactly what changed.

This animation ensures you never lose visual context during a correction.

Confirmation before export

Before copying or downloading, gōster shows a confirmation window with a liability notice.

When pasting into Word

The report is pasted with Arial 9pt font, preserved cell colors, and professional spacing.

Automatic diagnostic impression

gōster always generates the Diagnostic Impression section at the end of the report, ordered from highest to lowest clinical relevance (most severe finding first). To formulate each conclusion, it follows a three-step intelligent process:

  1. Reverse matching against the dictionary — It compares each description in the body of the report against the dictionary descriptions. If it matches, it uses the corresponding trigger as the conclusion. Example: if the body says "signal changes in the subcortical insertion of the anterior root of the lateral meniscus" and the dictionary has that description associated with the trigger "enthesopathy of the anterior root of the lateral meniscus", the conclusion will be exactly that trigger.
  2. Adaptive matching — If the same pathology appears in a different anatomical structure (medial/lateral, right/left, L4/L5, etc.), gōster adapts the dictionary trigger by changing only the location. Example: the dictionary has "enthesopathy anterior root lateral meniscus", but the finding is in the medial meniscus; the conclusion adapts to "enthesopathy of the anterior root of the medial meniscus".
  3. Clinical inference — If there is no match in the dictionary, gōster uses radiological knowledge to infer the diagnosis and transform the description into a precise clinical conclusion.
Note The impression is generated from the already-expanded report body, not from the direct dictation. The more complete your dictionary, the more accurate the conclusions.

Intelligent diagnostic normalization

The conclusions are not an echo of the description: they are a diagnosis. gōster automatically transforms descriptive morphological features (thinning, hyperintensity, discontinuity...) into their preferred final diagnostic form, provided that equivalence already exists in your dictionary, history, or previous corrections.

Example:

  • Description: "Asymmetric retinacula due to thinning of the medial one."
  • Incorrect conclusion: "Thinning of the medial retinaculum" (repeats the descriptive level).
  • Correct conclusion: "Laxity of the medial retinaculum/medial patellofemoral ligament" (applies the learned diagnostic equivalence).

gōster follows this priority order to decide the diagnostic form:

  1. Active dictionary — if the finding has an explicit trigger/description equivalence.
  2. Report history — if in previous reports of the same study type that finding has already been transformed in a specific way.
  3. Previous corrections — if you have previously corrected that formulation, it becomes a future rule.
  4. General radiological knowledge — only if no prior equivalence exists.
Tip The more you use gōster and the more corrections you save, the better it learns your preferred diagnostic equivalences. Over time, the conclusions become increasingly accurate and aligned with your clinical style.

Study history

gōster automatically saves each generated report and displays it as an intelligent clinical index.

Quick identification

Each history entry shows three lines:

  • Abbreviated study type — e.g.: MRI Knee, CT Urogram.
  • Key diagnostic phrase — A 2-6 word phrase automatically generated that describes the main finding. E.g.: left urolithiasis, partial ACL tear, L4-L5 disc herniation.
  • Date — Day and month of the study.

This lets you identify any study in seconds, without needing to open each report.

Dynamic update

The key phrase is automatically generated when the report is saved and is updated when you correct a report, always reflecting the final version of the diagnosis. If there is no pathology, it will show "no pathological findings".

  • Click on any entry to load that report.
  • Trash icon to delete an entry.

The more you use it, the better it works

gōster is not a static system. It is an assistant that evolves with you. Every report you generate, every correction you save, and every dictionary you build feeds a cycle of continuous improvement that makes your results increasingly accurate, faster, and more aligned with the way you work.

How gōster improves with use

  • Corrections that become rules. Every time you edit a report and click "Save changes", gōster extracts writing patterns and applies them automatically in future reports of the same study type. What you correct today, you will no longer have to touch tomorrow.
  • A dictionary that grows and enriches itself. gōster can automatically incorporate new entries into the dictionary from your corrections. Your clinical vocabulary expands with use, with no extra effort.
  • History as active memory. Your previous reports are not just reference: they are active memory that modifies the model's behavior. gōster identifies your writing style, your diagnostic equivalences, and your prior decisions.
  • Increasingly intelligent conclusions. With each correction, gōster learns your preferred diagnostic equivalences (description to conclusion), your usual classifications (A.M.A., Stoller, ICRS...), and your way of prioritizing findings.
  • Transfer of learning. When gōster detects similar findings or equivalent clinical structures in a new report, it automatically applies the style corrected in previous cases. A correction in one study transfers to all future analogous cases.
  • Coherence that fine-tunes itself. gōster internally validates each paragraph to avoid contradictions, and with accumulated learning, the need for corrections progressively decreases.
In summary The first report is good. The tenth is better. The hundredth is yours. gōster adapts to you, not the other way around. The more you use it, the less you will have to correct, and the more time you can devote to what really matters: analyzing your studies in depth.

Pair phone via QR

gōster lets you pair your phone with your desktop session by scanning a QR code. Once paired, you can dictate from the phone and see the generated report in real time on the computer — ideal when working at a station without a microphone or when you prefer the comfort of your own device.

How to pair your phone

  1. Open gōster on your computer and make sure you are logged in.
  2. In the left sidebar, click the QR code icon (it appears as a square with a dot pattern).
  3. A panel opens with a unique QR code generated for your active session. The code is valid for 5 minutes; after that time it regenerates automatically.
  4. On your phone, open the browser (Chrome or Safari recommended) or the camera app.
  5. Point the camera at the QR code and tap the link that appears on screen. This will open gōster on the phone, already authenticated with your account.
  6. Confirm the pairing: on the computer, a message "Phone paired successfully" will appear and the status icon will change to green.
Tip No app installation is required. gōster runs directly from the phone's browser.

Dictate from the phone

Once paired, the phone interface shows a large, centered microphone button. Use it exactly as you would on the computer:

  • Tap Start dictation and speak normally.
  • The transcript appears in real time on both the phone and the computer.
  • Tap Stop when you finish. gōster will generate the report automatically on the computer.

Pairing security

  • Each QR code is single-use and expires after 5 minutes.
  • The pairing remains active throughout your session. When you log out on the computer, access from the phone is disconnected automatically.
  • Do not share the QR code: whoever scans it will have access to your active session.
Note The computer and the phone must be on the same network, or the phone must have mobile data active, to maintain real-time synchronization.
